# Qamis
**Intro**
When Qis’mos and the Artklan Empire signed a pact to march on Edrad, depose the squabbling Revolutionary Council and restore the Keyberos dynasty to the throne, bookmakers in Kard set the over/under for the length of the war at sixty days.

The war lasted for thirty years.

But the war is over. The dragon Ryraxos, who delivered Edrad from defeat when it seemed inevitable, and then waged aggressive war across the continent for two and a half decades, is gone—safely exiled to our second moon. The Keyberos dynasty again rules Edrad as they have for four hundred years, their borders driven back to their historical lines. Artkos’s supremacy over the central continent is restored. Everything has been put back the way it was.

Except everything has changed. 

The high kings of Artkos see in the new Artklan League a pale shadow of their old empire while their nominal subjects cling to a tenuous quasi-independence. The Keyberos dynasty—and everyone else, for that matter—find that it is easy to sign an edict reversing thirty years of change, but harder to enforce it. Forthyr, after decades of neglecting the affairs of the southwest, find them impossible to ignore. And the drow, recruited as allies by Artkos in a desperation move, remain on the surface in a dozen scattered enclaves—most prominently Qama, the holy city of Bahumat.

## Language
- There are no racial languages — no elvish, dwarvish, etc
- Q is pronounced “Kh”
- Old Draconic (or Old Qis) is the language of scholarly publications and treaties - the “q without u”  that appears in many place names is from Old Draconic
- The main languages are national:
    - Edran
    - Artklan
    - Qis
    - Forthrian
    - Islas
- These languages are not standardized, however, and contain major regional variations. For example, most speakers from the capital of Artkos would have trouble understanding the Artklan of a speaker from Rathakos. So without qualification, the languages will be understood as meaning the prestige dialect of the language spoken in the capital and by the ruling class.
- For your starting language, assume you can speak the capital dialect and one regional dialect

# Nations
## Edrad

Historically the most politically and economically dominant nation in Qamis, Edrad is ruled by a half-elven nobility from the coastal capital city of Jia. The Keyberos dynasty has reigned over Edrad for four centuries—with a recent interregnum at the hands of first the Revolutionary Council and second the dragon overlord Ryraxos.

The capital city, Jia, sits on a peninsula jutting out into the Central Sea. 
It is by far the most culturally influential city in Qamis.

Edran nobility is half-elven and must be between one quarter and three quarters elf.

**On Ryraxos**
Contrary to what “dragon overlord” may lead one to think, Ryraxos is not truly that impressive of a dragon. Ryraxos is bronze and relatively young (perhaps sixty). Formidable, certainly, but less so than a competent adventuring party or a well-commanded artillery battery. Indeed, Ryraxos’s rapid rise to power began with his exploits as an artillery commander—dragons make excellent artillery officers, because they can help carry the guns! Like all dragons, Ryraxos can shape shift. Upon his defeat, he accepted exile to the second moon, and as such will play no role in the campaign.

## The Artklan League

The Artklan Empire used to rule over four tenths of the continent as well as exerting substantial influence over Qis’mos. 

**Artkos**
Governed directly by High King Ezevir Arth, Artkos yearns to restore its dominance over the rest of the Artklan League.

The historic heart of Artkos is the northwestern territory of West Artkos, but Artkos also includes a number of exclaves, including the central one containing the capital city of Ath’r, which has been the seat of central Qamisi empires since well before Artkos conquered it two hundred years ago.

Its southwestern enclave is centered on the city of Kard, a major financial and shipping center. Governance is Kard is fragile, as much of the population is Edran-speaking and welcomed the invasion twenty-eight years ago.

**Rathakos**
A mountainous border state along the Red Mountains bordering Edrad. Its capital, [Rath](https://www.msully.net/stuff/rath.png), sits in the northern foothills.

*This is where I’ll expect the campaign to start.*

Bordering Edrad, Qis’mos, and Artkos (as well as Qirkos, Lock, and Acrokos), Rathakos is attempting to hang on to a tenuous independence.

The Red Mountains remain one of the most dangerous and underexplored regions in the interior of Qamis, and so Rathakos has a strong tradition of adventuring and frontiersmanship. 

Made a Princedom by the Settlements of Ath’r, Rathakos has yet to find a Prince. It is currently governed by Regent John Dunne while a Grand Council deliberates on finding a monarch. In the mean time, Rathakos already suffers from internal unrest in many places, especially in the Southern Reaches annexed from Edrad and from mountain communities that still follow the old dragon cults.

Like essentially every nation other than Edrad and Artkos, Rathakos fought on both sides of the war at different times. After Ryraxos dissolved the Artklan Empire, the former mountain provinces—under Edran occupation—were organized into the Red Mountain Republic. When guerrilla resistance and reverses elsewhere forced an Edran withdrawal, the opposition faction of the Red Mountain Assembly purged the pro-Edran faction and established the new nations of Rathakos and Acrokos.

## Qis’mos

The heartland of Qis’mos is some of the richest and most productive land in Qamis, leaving it one of the most fought over regions during the war.

While Qis’mos was not actually the seat of the old Draconic empire, it is where the institutions of the empire persisted the longest after the dragons departed. A modified remnant of these institutions still survive, as Qis’mos is governed by a Potentate elected by—and impeachable by—the nobility, clergy, and guildmasters.

The Church of Bahamut has a enormous presence in Qis’mos, with their traditional seat in the holy city of Qama. Qama was occupied by the drow during the late phases of the Ryraxian wars, and while they have not despoiled it, they have appointed their own high priest, Qe’lth IV. This has led to a schism, with an alternate candidate (Qachu II) operating from Qis, albeit without any of the relics of office.

As the name suggests, the Qis language is the most similar to the Old Qis language that serves as the language of the academy and diplomacy.


## Forthyr

Forthyr is an enormous nation in the northeast of Qamis. It is ruled by Dragoncaller Shanlong VI, the latest in a line of sorcerer kings. The dynasty used to be dragonborn, before bloodlines ran thin.

Forthyr is somewhat backwards, and a prominent religious sect calling for the return of the Dragon Emperors receives tacit encouragement, if not support, from the Dragoncaller dynasty.


## Islands

The islands of the central sea are populated primarily from exiles from both continents. They are a thassolocracy of nautical robber barons who sell their powerful mercenary navies to continental powers.

There is a narrow wizard-made isthmus that crosses the central sea near its northern end, helped by two enormous bridges.
